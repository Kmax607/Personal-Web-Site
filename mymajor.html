My Major

I major in Human-Centered Design and Development at The Pennsylvania State University in the school of Information Sciences and Technology

Program Overview

The Human-Centered Design and Development major (HCDD) offered by the College of Information Sciences and Technology provides students with a comprehensive understanding of fundamental concepts and cutting-edge skills in leveraging technology to enhance human experiences. The program emphasizes learning outcomes aimed at:

  •   Identifying opportunities to integrate technology seamlessly into human activities.
  •   Designing and developing technology solutions that are both practical and user-friendly.
  •   Evaluating and refining designed technologies within real-world contexts.

Graduates of the HCDD program are equipped for successful careers in various sectors, including industry, government, and education. They play a crucial role in ensuring that technological advancements align with human needs, aspirations, and limitations.

The HCDD major adopts an interdisciplinary approach, combining essential coursework in mathematics, statistics, information technology, and application development with specialized studies in the social and psychological aspects of technology use. It offers courses in usability engineering, user research methods, and user interface design, augmenting the user-centered analysis and design concepts essential for HCDD professionals.

What is Human-Centered Design and Development?

Human-Centered Design and Development focuses on the holistic study of identifying, designing, constructing, and evaluating technologies to improve people's lives. It delves into understanding human behaviors and their interactions with technology, employing various methodologies and tools to develop effective technology solutions. The field encompasses collaboration with end-users and clients to grasp their needs and contexts, leading to the creation of impactful products and services. Human-centered design and development integrate principles from design thinking, human-computer interaction (HCI), interaction design, and user experience design with software development techniques.

Is This the Right Fit for You?

You might also be interested in HCDD if:

  •   You are passionate about crafting interactive technologies.
  •   You aspire to design, develop, and assess web, mobile, and other software applications.
  •   You enjoy collaborating with individuals to understand their lifestyles and how technology complements them.
  •   You are interested in planning, executing, and interpreting data from user studies.
  •   You are comfortable with uncertainty and embrace the iterative process, viewing failure as a stepping stone to success.
